The West

The West: A Medieval Powerhouse

The West stands as an enduring symbol of medieval might, leaving an indelible mark on history. Its legacy is one of conquests, strategy, and a ruler who ventured into the realm of the supernatural.

Founder and Legacy

Founded in 1491, The West was led by King Kanes, a ruler who wielded authority with an unwavering grip. His reign is a complex tapestry woven with power, ambition, and a haunting sacrifice.

Conquests and Dominion

The West's rise was defined by a formidable army that embarked on relentless campaigns of conquest. Medieval cities and towns fell under its dominion, yielding to its formidable forces.

The Dark Sacrifice

Kanes, the King of The West, made a chilling pact to gain supernatural powers and unmatched strength. He sacrificed his own wife and children, a grim ritual that bestowed upon him the power of undeath and unyielding might.

A Reign of Invasion

Empowered by dark forces, Kanes led his undead army on a spree of invasions. The West's forces, commanded by the now-undead king, swept through medieval realms, conquering lands and establishing dominion through fear and force.

The Enigma Endures

The story of The West, King Kanes, and his malevolent transformation into an undead ruler continues to captivate historians and storytellers. It stands as a testament to the complex interplay of power and sacrifice that defined an era of medieval conquests.
